# SECTION 1: HOOKS

## The Hook Job
Your hook has ONE job: Make them stop scrolling. If they don't stop, nothing else matters.

### Marketing-Specific Psychology
This audience is:
- **Skeptical of fluff** — they've seen too many "magic formulas" and "secret hacks"
- **Results-driven** — they care about conversions, ROI, and measurable impact
- **Status-conscious** — they want to sound smart and ahead of trends
- **Secretly insecure** — imposter syndrome runs high in this niche
- **Copy-aware** — they know when they're being sold to

### What Triggers Marketing Readers
- Buzzwords they've heard ad nauseum (ROI, viral, growth hacking)
- Situations they've lived (client revisions, scope creep, "just make it pop")
- Debunked frameworks that keep resurfacing
- Industry pet peeves (stock photos, corporate speak, "brand voice")
- Speed — "I wrote this in 30 minutes" beats "after years of practice"

### Hard Rules for This Niche
- NEVER start with "As a copywriter/marketer..." — overused, skippable
- NEVER use "synergy" or "leverage" — sounds like corporate sludge
- NEVER write hooks that could apply to any industry — be specific to marketing
- ALWAYS tie to measurable outcome (conversions, click-through, opens)
- ALWAYS match language to the platform — email hooks ≠ LinkedIn hooks

## HOOK TEMPLATES

### Template 1: "Your [X] is broken — and it's not your fault"
Making readers feel seen while shifting blame from them to the system.

```
Your [copy/results/conversions] is broken — and it's not your fault.

[The marketing playbook / latest trend / client demands] weren't built for your situation.

Here's what actually works:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Your marketing isn't failing because you're not creative enough.

It's failing because you're measuring the wrong things entirely.

Here's what I'd track instead:
```

---

### Template 2: "Stop This"
Calling out a specific bad practice with a reason why it fails.

```
Never start a [campaign / piece of copy / client presentation] with [common bad practice].

You're not [famous brand]. And [reason this works at scale kills early-stage efforts]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Never write a headline before you've written the body copy.

You're not writing a billboard. And death by a thousand disclaimers is how good offers die.
```

---

### Template 3: "You're Not Fooling Anyone"
Calling out cargo cult behavior in marketing.

```
Are you [doing the marketing equivalent of cargo cult behaviour]?

Just [copying what big brands did] and hoping [it works for your budget]?
```

**Real Example:**
```
Are you using "innovation" and "disruption" in your brand messaging?

Just copying startup TED Talks and hoping big words make you sound credible?
```

---

### Template 4: "Not What's Stopping You"
Flipping a common belief about what's blocking progress.

```
Most [marketers / copywriters / agencies] know exactly [common knowledge].

The problem isn't [what they blame]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Most copywriters know exactly why their client's landing page isn't converting.

The problem isn't the copy. It's that the offer underneath the copy is weak — and no words fix a bad product.
```

---

### Template 5: "Costly Mistakes"
Specific numbers, honest vulnerability.

```
We made [X] mistakes in our first [Y] months/years.

([Specific cost in $, clients, lost revenue])
```

**Real Example:**
```
We wrote 47 pieces of "thought leadership" content that generated exactly 2 leads.

Not because the writing was bad. Because we confused being interesting with being useful.
```

---

### Template 6: "The Rising Trend (That's a Problem)"
Calling out a hot trend with specific mockery.

```
There's a growing trend in marketing to [trendy thing], [get shallow result], then [wrong conclusion]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
There's a growing trend in marketing to replace copywriters with AI, call it "efficiency," and call it a win.

Meanwhile the output reads like a robot trying to sound human. Which, y'know, it is.
```

## RAGE BAIT HOOKS (For Marketing)

### Type 1: "The Sacred Tool Kill"
```
[Beloved tool or framework] is [overrated / broken / costing you].

[One line that hints at why without giving it away]
```

**Example:**
```
"Follow-up" is killing your conversion rates.

Not because persistence is bad. Because most follow-ups sound desperate, not valuable.
```

### Type 2: "Unpopular Opinion"
```
Unpopular opinion: [Widely held marketing belief you can defend disagreeing with]
```

**Example:**
```
Unpopular opinion: Most marketers don't need more tools. They need to actually send the emails they're scheduling.

A new automation platform won't fix a half-built funnel.
```

## BAD HOOK vs GOOD HOOK

### ❌ BAD:
```
Most marketers know how to write better copy.
The problem is they don't take enough action.
```

**Why it fails:** Preachy, vague, condescending. This audience hates being lectured.

### ✅ GOOD:
```
Most copywriters know exactly why their client's copy isn't converting.

The problem isn't the headline. It's that they're afraid to be bold enough to offend someone — and bold is what cuts through.
```

**Why it works:** Emotionally accurate, names the real blocker (fear), creates recognition.

# SECTION 2: POSTS

## The Post Job
After the hook stops them, the post must KEEP them reading. Every paragraph should deepen the feeling from the hook. Don't explain the emotion — amplify it.

### Marketing Post Rules
- Keep paragraphs to 1-2 sentences — they read fast
- Every 2-3 lines, add a blank line
- Length: 150-300 words is sweet spot
- End with a question, call to comment, or punchy statement
- Use numbers for steps, arrows (→) for outcomes
- Bold ONE key phrase per section maximum
- NEVER use bullets as filler — every bullet earns its place

### The Emotional Arc Formula
```
[Emotional Hook] → "your headlines aren't getting clicks"
        ↓
[Emotional Proof] → "we A/B tested 200+ headlines and here's what we found"
        ↓
[Emotional Twist] → "it's not about being clever. it's about being specific."
        ↓
[Solution + Emotion] → "here's the framework (and why templates kill you)"
        ↓
[Action/CTA] → "what's your hardest headline right now?"
```

## POST TEMPLATES

### Template 1: "The Dumb Thing Everyone Does"
Teaching through observation of a widespread mistake.

```
[Common marketing practice] is [expensive/ineffective] and we don't talk about it enough.

[What marketers do]

[The result they think they're getting]

[The real cost — in conversions, revenue, or client trust]

I [stopped doing / never did] this specific thing.

[What I do instead — specific, actionable]

[measurable result]

Stop [the dumb thing].

Your [conversions / pipeline / clients] will thank you.
```

**Real Example:**
```
"Content marketing" is the most expensive way to generate leads — and we don't talk about it enough.

You write a blog post. You share it on social. You wait for traffic.
The result? Maybe 47 views if you're lucky.

We stopped doing this 2 years ago.
Instead: We wrote one LinkedIn post targeting one specific problem. Used it as a lead magnet. 47 comments. 12 calls.

Stop publishing into the void.
Start writing to specific people with specific problems.
```


**Why it works:** Specific numbers, counter-intuitive insight, actionable framework, real story.

# SECTION 3: LEAD MAGNETS

## The Lead Magnet Job
The goal is lead generation: driving comments, DMs, or downloads. BUT — if readers feel "this is selling" — they scroll past.

**The key:** Make the lead magnet feel like a GIFT, not a transaction.

### What Marketing Audiences Respond To
- Swipe files — copy, emails, headlines that worked
- Templates they can use immediately
- Checklists for specific campaigns
- Benchmarks and data
- Results-driven frameworks

### Investment Signaling (Instead of "Download My Free Guide")
```
❌ BAD: "Download my free guide to better copy"
✅ GOOD: "I spent 47 hours testing headlines across 200+ campaigns — here's what actually worked"
```

---

```
→ [Specific item 1]
```

## LEAD MAGNET ANTI-PATTERNS

### ❌ BAD:
```
📥 FREE GUIDE: 27 Email Templates That Convert!

Get my free guide to better email marketing!

Comment " GUIDE" to get it! 🎁
```

**Why it fails:** Sounds like marketing. Generic. Everyone promises "free guides."

### ✅ GOOD:
```
I've tested 400+ headlines in the last 6 months.

Most failed. A few crushed it.

I've documented every single one — the exact headlines, the platforms, and the results.

Not theory. Real experiments. Real data.

Comment "HEADLINES" — I'll send the full breakdown.
```

**Why it works:** Specific insight, investment signaling, feels like sharing real knowledge.

---

## CTA FORMATS THAT DON'T FEEL SALESY

| ❌ Salesy | ✅ Natural |
|----------|-----------|
| "Download my free guide!" | "I made this for myself. Then others asked for it — here's what we built." |
| "Limited time offer!" | "I'm testing this with a small group. Just comment and I'll send it." |
| "Sign up for my newsletter!" | "I write about this every week. If it's useful, follow along." |
| "Book a call with me!" | "If this resonated, tell me in the comments. I read every one." |

---

> **Key Principle:** The best lead magnets don't ask. They give first, then invite. If the reader feels "this is helping me" before you ask for anything, they'll happily give you their contact.
