Use this file when the goal is **lead generation** for a SaaS or tech audience — driving demo requests, newsletter signups, resource downloads, or DMs from founders, PMs, and operators.

## AGENT INSTRUCTIONS (DETAILED)

### What SaaS audiences respond to in lead magnets
- **Templates they can use immediately** (not "frameworks" — actual templates)
- **Real data** — benchmarks, cohort data, churn numbers, conversion rates
- **Systems and processes** — exact workflows, not general principles
- **Swipe files** — copy, emails, onboarding sequences that worked
- **Calculators** — LTV, CAC payback, churn impact — anything with a number at the end

### CTA strategy for SaaS audience
- SaaS people are skeptical of "comment WORD" CTAs — they feel like manipulation
- Offset this with: **specificity of the resource + credibility of the proof**
- Best CTA format: "Comment [WORD] and I'll DM you the [specific resource name]"
- Alternative: "Link in the comments" (lower friction but lower conversion)
- Always mention format: "12-page PDF", "Notion template", "Google Sheet calculator"

## THE PSYCHOLOGY OF VIRAL LEAD MAGNETS

### Why Regular Lead Magnets Fail
Most lead magnets fail because they're built on the **value promise model**: "get my free guide to X." The problem? Everyone promises value, nobody believes it anymore. We've all downloaded "free guides" that were thinly-veiled sales pitches with zero actual insight.

### Investment Signaling: The New Model
The new model isn't about promising value — it's about *proving* value through visible effort.

When you say "I spent 27 hours creating this resource," you're proving you've invested real time. That creates an asymmetry that triggers reciprocity psychology. The reader thinks: "if they spent 27 hours on this, it must be good. And they're giving it away for free. I should at least check it out."

**Investment signaling examples:**
- "the 43-page playbook I built over 18 months working with 32 B2B companies"
- "the exact swipe file of 156 hooks that generated 2M+ impressions this year"
- "the 3-hour video walkthrough I recorded for my $15k clients (now free)"

### The Lead Magnet Emotional Stack
The highest-performing lead magnets stack multiple emotional triggers:

1. **Curiosity Gap (hook)**
   "the LinkedIn post structure that books 15-20 calls per week without any cold outreach"

2. **Identity Validation (problem statement)**
   "if you've been posting consistently for months and still hearing crickets, it's not your fault. You've been taught to optimize for the algorithm when you should be optimizing for psychology."

3. **Tribal Belonging (us vs them)**
   "most LinkedIn 'experts' teach tactics. engagement pods, posting times, hashtag strategies. That's cargo cult marketing. The founders booking 50+ calls per month aren't doing any of that."

4. **Investment Signaling (credibility)**
   "I've analyzed 2,400+ viral posts and spent 8 months building this framework. It's the same system that helped one client go from 0 to $48k MRR in 90 days."

5. **Aspiration + Believability (offer)**
   "I've turned it into a complete playbook: the psychology, the frameworks, the exact templates. Comment 'VIRAL' and I'll send it over."

### The FOMO Stack
The psychological sequence that creates maximum lead magnet engagement:

1. **Social Proof + Specificity**
   "I've sent this to 847 founders this month and the feedback has been wild."

2. **Transformation Story**
   "One founder used the framework and booked 23 calls in his first week. Another scaled her agency from $15k to $42k MRR in 60 days."

3. **Exclusivity Signal**
   "I normally only share this with clients paying $3k/month. But I want to test something."

4. **Action Urgency**
   "Comment 'SYSTEM' below and I'll send it over. I read every comment so might take a few hours if this post does well."

### The Lead Magnet CTA Psychology
For SaaS:
- "Comment [WORD] and I'll DM you the [specific resource]"
- Format specificity is crucial — "12-page Notion template" vs "free guide"
- Credibility via proof: specific results, client names, before/after
