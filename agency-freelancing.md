# SECTION 1: HOOKS

## The Hook Job
Your hook has ONE job: Make them stop scrolling. If they don't stop, nothing else matters.

### Agency/Freelance-Specific Psychology
This audience is:
- **Dealing with reality** — feast-or-famine revenue, scope creep, underpricing
- **Positioning-obsessed** — "I do a bit of everything" traps them
- **Skeptical of advice** — they've heard "know your worth" a thousand times
- **Looking for permission** — to charge more, say no, fire clients
- **Tired of hustle-bro energy** — they want calm professionalism

### What Triggers This Audience
- Specific dollar/revenue numbers
- Relatable client horror stories (carefully handled)
- Permission to charge more, say no, fire clients
- Systems and processes that reduce chaos
- "I wish someone had told me this" framing

### Hard Rules for This Niche
- NEVER be vague about the niche (freelancer ≠ agency owner ≠ consultant)
- ALWAYS use a revenue number if available — it's the universal credibility signal
- Avoid lecturing — this audience has heard advice a thousand times
- The most powerful posts are honest stories, not frameworks
- This audience is tired of hype — they want calm, specific, honest

## HOOK TEMPLATES

### Template 1: "Your [X] is broken — and it's not your fault"
Making readers feel seen while shifting blame to systems.

```
Your [pricing / positioning / pipeline / client relationship] is broken — and it's not your fault.

[The industry / common advice / how everyone starts out] set you up for this.

Here's what nobody tells you until it's already cost you [something painful]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Your rates are too low — and it's not because you lack confidence.

It's because every freelancing resource tells you to "research the market rate" without telling you the market rate is artificially low because everyone else is doing the same thing.
```

---

### Template 2: "Stop This"
Calling out a specific bad practice with reason.

```
Never [specific destructive behavior].

```

**Real Example:**
```
Never send a proposal without a discovery call first.

You're not a vending machine. And clients who skip the conversation almost always have the wrong expectations before the project starts.
```

## BAD HOOK vs GOOD HOOK

### ❌ BAD:
```
Most freelancers know they should raise their rates.
The problem is they just don't do it.
```

**Why it fails:** "Just do it" is not insight. It's the thing they already know and aren't doing.

### ✅ GOOD:
```
Most freelancers know their rates are too low.

The problem isn't confidence. It's that raising rates requires having a conversation — and most freelancers were never taught how to have that conversation without losing the client.
```

**Why it works:** Names the real specific obstacle (conversation skill, not confidence), feels like understanding, not lecturing.

# SECTION 2: POSTS

## The Post Job
After the hook stops them, the post must KEEP them reading. Every paragraph should deepen the feeling from the hook. Don't explain the emotion — amplify it.

### Agency Post Rules
- Specific numbers — lost revenue, gained revenue, hours, clients
- Honest war stories — the mistake, the cost, the fix, the lesson
- Permission posts — giving the reader permission to do the thing they're afraid to do
- Practical frameworks — not "think bigger" advice, actual process
- Avoid: success porn without context, client shaming (even anonymous), vague advice
- Voice: battle-tested operator who made the mistake, fixed it, wants others to skip the pain

# SECTION 3: LEAD MAGNETS

## The Lead Magnet Job
The goal is lead generation: driving comments, DMs, or downloads. BUT — if readers feel "this is selling" — they scroll past.

**The key:** Make the lead magnet feel like a GIFT, not a transaction.

### What Agency/Freelance Audiences Respond To
- Contract templates — they will always want these
- Pricing calculators — how to set rates, calculate margin
- Client process docs — onboarding, offboarding, scope management
- Scripts — for rate increases, scope creep, client rejection
- Revenue breakdowns — "how I structure a $15k/month retainer"

### Investment Signaling (Instead of "Download My Free Guide")
```
❌ BAD: "Download my free proposal template"
✅ GOOD: "I refined this template over 4 years of £200k+ in closes — here's the version that finally worked"
```

```

Comment " TEMPLATE" to get it! 🎁
```

**Why it fails:** Sounds like marketing. Generic. This audience knows the game.

### ✅ GOOD:
```
I lost £94,000 in unbilled work before I built this system.

Not from bad clients. From never having a conversation about money.

I've turned that learning into a template — the exact system I use to price, propose, and close.

No theory. Just what actually works after 4 years of mistakes.

Comment "SYSTEM" — I'll send it over.
```

**Why it works:** Specific numbers, honest story, specific value, feels like a peer sharing knowledge.

## CTA FORMATS THAT DON'T FEEL SALESY

| ❌ Salesy | ✅ Natural |
|----------|-----------|
| "Download my free template!" | "I built this for myself. Others asked for it — here's what works." |
| "Limited time offer!" | "I'm testing this with a small group. Just comment and I'll send it." |
| "Book a strategy call!" | "If this resonated, tell me in the comments. I read every one." |
| "Join my agency mastermind!" | "If this is useful, I'll share more. Just let me know what resonates." |

---

> **Key Principle:** The best lead magnets don't ask. They give first, then invite. If the reader feels "this is helping me" before you ask for anything, they'll happily give you their contact. This audience is smart about marketing — be specific, be real, don't play games.
