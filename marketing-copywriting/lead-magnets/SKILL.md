# LinkedIn Lead Magnet Post Templates — Marketing & Copywriting

## SKILL OVERVIEW
Use this file when the goal of the post is **lead generation** — driving comments, DMs, or downloads of a resource. These posts have a CTA built into the structure. They are NOT pure value posts — they exist to convert attention into contacts.

Always load `marketing-copywriting/hooks/SKILL.md` first.

---

## AGENT INSTRUCTIONS (DETAILED)

### Step 1 — Define the magnet before writing the post
The post is packaging. The magnet is the product. Know these before you write a word:
- **What is the resource?** (checklist, template, playbook, swipe file, guide, video)
- **What specific outcome does it deliver?**
- **Who exactly is it for?** (B2B copywriters? Email marketers? SaaS marketers?)
- **What's the CTA trigger word?** (Comment "SWIPE" / Comment "COPY" / Comment "SEND IT")

### Step 2 — The lead magnet post formula
Every high-converting lead magnet post has these 5 elements:
1. **Hook** — stops the scroll (load hooks skill)
2. **Credibility line** — why YOU have this resource worth getting
3. **Pain agitation** — what they're suffering without it
4. **Tease of contents** — what's inside (4-6 bullets max)
5. **CTA** — clear, single action, with trigger word

### Step 3 — CTA rules
- ONE action only. Not "like, comment, AND connect."
- Give a trigger word — "Comment [WORD]" outperforms "DM me"
- Add scarcity if you mean it ("first 50 people") — never fake it
- Always add "must be connected to receive" — drives connection requests

### Step 4 — Tone for lead magnets
More authoritative than regular posts. You are the expert delivering the thing. Confidence without arrogance.

## THE PSYCHOLOGY OF VIRAL LEAD MAGNETS

### Why Regular Lead Magnets Fail
Most lead magnets fail because they're built on the **value promise model**: "get my free guide to X." The problem? Everyone promises value, nobody believes it anymore. We've all downloaded "free guides" that were thinly-veiled sales pitches with zero actual insight.

### Investment Signaling: The New Model
The new model isn't about promising value — it's about *proving* value through visible effort.

When you say "I spent 27 hours creating this resource," you're proving you've invested real time. That creates an asymmetry that triggers reciprocity psychology. The reader thinks: "if they spent 27 hours on this, it must be good. And they're giving it away for free. I should at least check it out."

**Investment signaling examples:**
- "the 47-page swipe file I've collected over 3 years testing headlines"
- "the exact email templates that generated $240k in pipeline this quarter"
- "the 2-hour video walkthrough I only do for $10k clients (now free)"

### The Lead Magnet Emotional Stack
The highest-performing lead magnets stack multiple emotional triggers:

1. **Curiosity Gap (hook)**
   "the headline formula that converts 3x better than anything you've tried"

2. **Identity Validation (problem statement)**
   "if you've been writing headlines that sound like everyone else's, it's not your fault. You learned from the same templates everyone else did."

3. **Tribal Belonging (us vs them)**
   "most 'copywriters' teach templates. The ones booking 30+ leads monthly from LinkedIn are writing something different."

4. **Investment Signaling (credibility)**
   "I've tested 400+ headlines in the last 6 months and documented every result. Here are the exact 12 that crushed it."

5. **Aspiration + Believability (offer)**
   "I've turned it into a swipe file: the exact headlines, the platforms they worked on, and why. Comment 'HEADLINES' and I'll send it over."

### The FOMO Stack
The psychological sequence that creates maximum lead magnet engagement:

1. **Social Proof + Specificity**
   "This swipe file has been sent to 600+ marketers. The response has been insane."

2. **Transformation Story**
   "One freelancer used these headlines and booked 12 discovery calls in a week. Another increased her email open rates by 40%."

3. **Exclusivity Signal**
   "I normally only share these inside my $2k/month retainer. But I'm testing something."

4. **Action Urgency**
   "Comment 'SWIPE' below and I'll DM it. Reading every comment so it might take a few hours if this does well."

### The Lead Magnet CTA Psychology
For marketing/copywriting:
- Trigger words that feel scarce: "SWIPE", "TEMPLATES", "SEND IT"
- Format specificity: "the exact Google Doc template" vs "free resource"
- Connection + DM combo drives network growth

## BAD vs GOOD LEAD MAGNET POST COMPARISONS

❌ **Bad:**
```
I made a free copywriting guide!

It has lots of tips on writing better copy.

Comment "GUIDE" and I'll DM you.
```
*Why it fails: No hook, no credibility, no pain point, no tease of contents, vague benefit*

✅ **Good:**
```
Most copywriters charge by the word.

The best ones charge by the result.

Here's the difference — and the exact framework I use to position every project for premium rates:

Inside my free Copy Pricing Playbook:

→ The 3 questions that tell you if a client will lowball you (before you send a proposal)

→ The email script that turned 4 "can you do it cheaper?" conversations into full-rate clients
→ How to talk about ROI when clients don't track marketing metrics

This isn't theory. I used this system to go from £50/hour to £300/hour in 14 months.

Comment "PRICING" + connect with me and I'll send it over.

(First 75 people only — I actually track this.)
```
*Why it works: Credibility, specific pain, specific contents, real proof, clear CTA*

## DO'S AND DON'TS

✅ **DO:**
- Name the resource clearly — "a free guide" is weaker than "my 22-page Copy Pricing Playbook"
- Give bullet points that are specific enough to create desire
- Include one piece of proof (client win, stat, timeframe)
- Use a trigger word that relates to the resource topic

❌ **DON'T:**
- Say "limited to 100 copies" if you're not tracking it — people can tell
- Bury the CTA at the end of a wall of text
- List 10+ things inside the resource — 4-6 bullets max
- Make the "comment" CTA longer than 2 steps
