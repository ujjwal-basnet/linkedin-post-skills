# SECTION 1: HOOKS

## The Hook Job
Your hook has ONE job: Make them stop scrolling. If they don't stop, nothing else matters.

### Coaching-Specific Psychology
This audience is:
- **Emotionally literate** — they respond to language that names feelings accurately
- **Self-aware but stuck** — they know they have a problem; looking for permission and clarity
- **Skeptical of gurus** — overselling creates instant distrust
- **Identity-driven** — they think in terms of "who I'm becoming," not just "what I'm doing"
- **Moved by transformation stories** — before/after is powerful here

### What Triggers Coaching Readers
- Precision emotion — the more specific the feeling named, the more they feel seen
- Permission — giving them permission to feel what they're already feeling
- Non-obvious reframes — renaming the problem so it becomes solvable
- Honest vulnerability — not performed, but earned openness
- Challenges to "just believe in yourself" culture

### Hard Rules for This Niche
- NEVER sound like a guru — "I'm going to teach you..." reads as salesy
- NEVER use toxic positivity — "Just believe in yourself!" triggers eye-rolls
- NEVER be vague — "trust the process" means nothing
- ALWAYS be specific about the transformation
- ALWAYS make it feel like a wise friend, not a coach

## HOOK TEMPLATES

### Template 1: "You're Not [Negative Self-Label]"
Making readers feel seen, shifting blame to external systems.

```
You're not [lazy / broken / not good enough / behind].

[External or psychological reason they're struggling].

Here's what's actually going on:
```

**Real Example:**
```
You're not lazy.

You're exhausted from pretending you're fine.

There's a difference — and the solution is completely different too.
```

---

### Template 2: "Stop Calling It..."
Reframing the diagnosis to remove shame.

```
Stop calling it [self-sabotage / procrastination / fear].

[What it's actually protecting you from].

That's worth understanding before you try to "fix" it.
```

**Real Example:**
```
Stop calling it procrastination.

It's not laziness. It's your nervous system protecting you from the judgment that might come if you try and fail.

That changes everything about how to move forward.
```

---

### Template 3: "Most People Don't Talk About..."
Opening taboo truths.

```
Most [coaches / people / high achievers] don't talk about [uncomfortable truth].

[Why it matters]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Most coaches don't talk about the clients they couldn't help.

And what that taught them about the limits of coaching.

Some things take longer than a 90-minute session. And that's not a failure.
```

---

### Template 4: "The Problem Isn't [X] — It's [Y]"
Flipping conventional wisdom.

```
Most [people / high achievers / leaders] know exactly [what they're supposed to do].

The problem isn't [what they blame].

It's [the real blocker]:
```

**Real Example:**
```
Most people know they need to set better boundaries.

The problem isn't that they don't know how.

It's that they've been taught that needing less makes you easier to love.
```

---

### Template 5: "You Need to Disappoint People"

**Real Example:**

Not because it feels good. Because saying yes to everything is making you excellent at other people's lives and exhausted in your own.

## BAD HOOK vs GOOD HOOK

### ❌ BAD:
```
You're struggling because nobody taught you how to handle your emotions.

Follow these steps to feel better.
```

**Why it fails:** Condescending, clinical, removes agency too completely. Sounds like a guru.

### ✅ GOOD:
```
You're not unmotivated. You're unclear.

Motivation isn't the problem. Ambiguity is.

And nobody teaches you how to get clear.
```

**Why it works:** Precise naming of feeling, immediate relief, creates curiosity, sounds like a wise friend.

# SECTION 2: POSTS

## The Post Job
After the hook stops them, the post must KEEP them reading. Every paragraph should deepen the feeling from the hook. Don't explain the emotion — amplify it.

### Coaching Post Rules
- Use "you" and "I" — both build intimacy
- Avoid: "As a coach, I see this all the time..." — sounds clinical
- Prefer: "Something I see again and again..." — more human
- Short sentences for emotional moments, longer for explanation
- The "too safe" trap: push the reframe further than feels comfortable

### The Transformation Arc
1. **Name the feeling precisely** — Go beyond "burnout" to "the exhaustion that comes from doing everything right and still feeling unfulfilled"
2. **Validate** — "That's not weakness. That's awareness."
3. **Reframe** — "What you're calling failure might be redirection."
4. **Invite** — "Here's what I'd instead..."

# SECTION 3: LEAD MAGNETS

## The Lead Magnet Job
The goal is lead generation: driving comments, DMs, or downloads. BUT — if readers feel "this is selling" — they scroll past.

**The key:** Make the lead magnet feel like a GIFT, not a transaction.

### What Coaching Audiences Respond To
- Self-assessment tools — quizzes, reflection prompts
- Workbooks — guided exercises they can do alone
- Mini-frameworks — named systems that make complex processes feel navigable
- Honest guides — "what nobody tells you about X"
- Scripts — for difficult conversations, boundary-setting

### Investment Signaling (Instead of "Download My Free Guide")
```
❌ BAD: "Download my free guide to setting boundaries"
✅ GOOD: "I spent 5 years perfecting this script with 200+ clients — here's the version I wish existed when I was stuck"
```

## LEAD MAGNET ANTI-PATTERNS

### ❌ BAD:
```
🎁 FREE GIFT: 21-Day Mindset Makeover!

Transform your life in just 21 days!

Comment " GIFT" to claim! ✨
```

**Why it fails:** Sounds like marketing. Overpromises. Generic. This audience can smell desperation.

### ✅ GOOD:
```
I made this because I wished it existed when I was stuck.

The reflection journal I used to figure out what I actually wanted — after 3 years of doing what I thought I "should" want.

Not motivation. NotManifestation. Just questions that cut through the noise.

Comment "JOURNAL" — I'll send it over. No catch.
```

**Why it works:** Authentic origin story, specific tool, feels like help not sales.

---

## CTA FORMATS THAT DON'T FEEL SALESY

| ❌ Salesy | ✅ Natural |
|----------|-----------|
| "Download my free guide!" | "I made this because I wished it existed when I needed it." |
| "Limited spots available!" | "I'll send it to everyone who comments — no catch." |
| "Book a clarity call!" | "If this resonated, tell me in the comments. I read every one." |
| "Join my program!" | "If this is useful, I'll share more. Just let me know what resonates." |

---

> **Key Principle:** The best lead magnets don't ask. They give first, then invite. If the reader feels "this is helping me" before you ask for anything, they'll happily give you their contact. In coaching, authenticity matters MORE because this audience is skeptical of guru energy.
